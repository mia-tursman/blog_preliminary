The Guinness Storehouse

☘︎

Museum and bar about how Guinness is made

Overview

  • Book tickets ahead of time (around 21 euros on weekdays and 27 euros on weekends)
  • Student discount
  • The ticket includes one free pint, and you can add another with your face printed for 6 euros
  • About 1.5-2 hours overall

My Experience

After a weekend in Galway, we took a bus to the Cliffs of Moher, on the west coast of Ireland. The bus ride was very windy and made me a little car sick, but it was worth it. After arriving, I walked along the cliffs and looked out over the Atlantic Ocean. It was very rainy and windy, but the views were beautiful, and it was one of the most memorable places I visited in Ireland. We visited the Guinness Storehouse in Dublin, which was a very cool and immersive museum. You start by learning about the ingredients and brewing process of Guinness beer, then move up to the marketing room and tasting room. There is also an option to have your face printed on a Guinness or learn how to pour one for an extra charge. Finally, you reach the top bar, which overlooks Dublin, and you are given a free pint. This was very cool, and I would definitely recommend it to Guinness lovers!
